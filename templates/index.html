Class Descriptions:

Early Blight: Early Blight is a common fungal disease caused by *Alternaria solani*, affecting leaves, stems, and fruits, characterized by dark, concentric rings on leaves.

Late Blight: Late Blight is caused by the pathogen *Phytophthora infestans* and can lead to severe crop losses, manifesting as water-soaked lesions, typically on leaves, stems, and fruits. It spreads quickly in humid conditions.

Healthy: Healthy potato plants show no signs of disease and have green, robust leaves and stems without any lesions, spots, or discoloration.
